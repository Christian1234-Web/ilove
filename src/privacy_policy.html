🔒 Your Privacy Matters

At Capatuno, we are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, share, and protect your information when you use our social finance platform.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide to Us

  • Account Information: Name, email address, phone number, date of birth, profile photo
  • Identity Verification: Government-issued ID, address verification documents
  • Financial Information: Bank account details, payment card information, transaction history
  • Communication Data: Messages, photos, and other content you share through our platform
  • Profile Information: Bio, interests, social connections, uploaded photos and posts
  • Support Communications: Information provided when contacting customer support

1.2 Information We Collect Automatically

  • Device Information: Device type, operating system, unique device identifiers
  • Usage Data: App features used, time spent on platform, interaction patterns
  • Location Data: IP address, general location (with your permission)
  • Technical Data: Log files, crash reports, performance metrics
  • Camera and Photos: Access to camera and photo library (with permission)

2. How We Use Your Information

2.1 Service Provision

  • Create and maintain your account
  • Process financial transactions and money transfers
  • Facilitate social interactions and messaging
  • Provide customer support and respond to inquiries
  • Verify your identity for security and compliance

2.2 Safety and Security

  • Detect and prevent fraud, abuse, and illegal activities
  • Enforce our Terms of Service and Community Guidelines
  • Protect the safety of our users, especially minors
  • Comply with legal obligations and regulatory requirements
  • Conduct security monitoring and risk assessment

2.3 Communication and Marketing

  • Send transactional notifications and service updates
  • Provide promotional communications (with your consent)
  • Deliver personalized content and recommendations
  • Conduct surveys and research to improve our services

3. Information Sharing and Disclosure

3.1 We Share Information With:

Entity Type Purpose Data Shared
Payment Processors Process financial transactions Payment details, transaction amounts
Identity Verification Services Verify user identity and prevent fraud Name, ID documents, address
Cloud Service Providers Data storage and platform hosting All user data (encrypted)
Analytics Services Improve app performance and user experience Usage data, device information
Customer Support Tools Provide technical support Support conversations, account details

3.2 Legal and Safety Disclosures

  • Law Enforcement: When required by law or to protect safety
  • Legal Proceedings: In response to court orders or legal processes
  • Child Safety: Reporting to NCMEC and relevant authorities as required
  • Emergency Situations: To prevent harm to users or the public

4. Data Security

4.1 Security Measures

  • Encryption: End-to-end encryption for sensitive communications
  • Secure Storage: Industry-standard encryption for data at rest
  • Access Controls: Role-based access with multi-factor authentication
  • Regular Audits: Security assessments and penetration testing
  • Incident Response: Procedures for detecting and responding to breaches

4.2 Financial Data Protection

  • PCI DSS compliance for payment card data
  • Tokenization of sensitive financial information
  • Secure API connections with banking partners
  • Regular security monitoring and fraud detection

5. Your Privacy Rights

5.1 Access and Control

  • Access: View and download your personal data
  • Correction: Update or correct inaccurate information
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your account and data
  • Portability: Export your data in a portable format
  • Restriction: Limit how we process your information

5.2 Communication Preferences

  • Opt out of marketing communications
  • Control push notification settings
  • Manage email preferences
  • Adjust privacy settings within the app

6. International Data Transfers

Capatuno operates globally and may transfer your information to countries outside your residence. We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for EU data transfers
  • Adequacy decisions where applicable
  • Additional security measures for sensitive data
  • Compliance with local data protection laws

7. Data Retention

7.1 Retention Periods

  • Account Data: Retained while your account is active
  • Transaction Records: 7 years for regulatory compliance
  • Communication Data: 2 years from last activity
  • Support Data: 3 years from resolution
  • Security Logs: 1 year for fraud prevention

7.2 Deletion Process

  • Account deletion removes most personal data within 30 days
  • Some data retained for legal/regulatory requirements
  • Anonymized data may be retained for analytics
  • Backup systems purged within 90 days

8. Children's Privacy

🛡️ Special Protections for Minors

  • Age Requirement: Users must be 13 or older
  • Parental Consent: Required for users under 16 in applicable jurisdictions
  • Limited Data Collection: Minimal data collection from users under 18
  • Enhanced Security: Additional protections for minor accounts
  • COPPA Compliance: Full compliance with Children's Online Privacy Protection Act

9. Cookies and Tracking

9.1 Types of Cookies

  • Essential Cookies: Required for basic app functionality
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand app usage
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your settings and preferences
  • Security Cookies: Protect against fraud and unauthorized access

9.2 Third-Party Tracking

  • Limited third-party analytics services
  • No advertising tracking without consent
  • Clear opt-out mechanisms available
  • Regular review of third-party practices

10. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or applicable laws. We will:

  • Notify users of material changes via email or in-app notification
  • Provide 30 days' notice before significant changes take effect
  • Maintain previous versions for reference
  • Update the "Last Modified" date at the top of this policy

11. Regional Privacy Rights

11.1 European Union (GDPR)

  • Legal basis for processing under GDPR Article 6
  • Right to object to processing
  • Right to lodge complaints with supervisory authorities
  • Data Protection Officer contact information available

11.2 California (CCPA)

  • Right to know what personal information is collected
  • Right to delete personal information
  • Right to opt-out of sale of personal information
  • Non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights

11.3 Other Jurisdictions

  • Compliance with applicable local privacy laws
  • Additional rights as required by local regulations
  • Local data residency requirements where applicable
